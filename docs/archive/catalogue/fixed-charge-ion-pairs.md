# Fixed-charge main-group ion pairs

ChemSpec generates binary ionic formula units from charge families rather than
from compound-specific reaction code. The supported fixed cations are Group 1
(`+1`), Group 2 (`+2`), and aluminium (`+3`). The supported monatomic anions
are halides (`-1`), oxide and sulfide (`-2`), and nitride and phosphide (`-3`).

For each charge pairing, the generator reduces the absolute charges by their
greatest common divisor. The resulting ratio controls all of the following:

- the product formula and balanced elemental equation;
- the number of explicit cation and anion components in the ionic graph;
- metallic-electron release and electron-transfer operations;
- charge-aware ionic association and simulation-frame placement;
- reactant-viewer recognition of the complete formula unit.

For example, the same rule machinery produces `LiF`, `MgF2`, `AlF3`, `Li3N`,
`Mg3N2`, and `AlN`. Elemental nonmetals retain their reviewed source topology:
`F2`, `Cl2`, `Br2`, `I2`, `O2`, `N2`, `S8`, and `P4`. Their covalent bonds are
cleaved by generalized operations before electron transfer and ionic
association. Equivalent operations are grouped by the guided-animation layer.

The current catalogue contains 81 reviewed ion-pair experiences generated by
22 reusable family rules. Variable-charge metals and polyatomic ions are not
part of this fixed-charge slice; they can be added as selectable charge or ion
families without changing the balancing or ionic-topology engine.
